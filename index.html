Way of the Cross

Through Downtown Raleigh (NC).

The idea of a Way of the Cross around the streets of Downtown Raleigh (NC) was born to help ourselves to be immersed in the Passion of Christ, that the Church invites us to meditate on Good Friday, and as a missionary gesture for the city we live in. The procession will make several stops throughout downtown Raleigh. At each station, we will follow the traditional readings from the Gospel and supplement them with choral songs and short passages from modern Catholic writers like Paul Claudel and Charles Peguy.

History

The origin of this gesture

The founder of Communion and Liberation, Servant of God Fr. Luigi Giussani (1922-2005), started proposing the Way of the Cross to college students, gathering them by the thousands in Caravaggio, Italy. To supplement the traditional readings from the Gospel, Fr. Giussani introduced short passages from some of the greatest contemporary Catholic writers, including Paul Claudel and Charles Peguy, who help to express the contemporary reverberations of Christâ€™s historic death and resurrection in our world. Fr. Giussani also asked that a choir accompany the procession, singing at every station to constantly reawaken the awareness of the faithful with the beauty and sorrow that only choral music can express.

The Way of the Cross across the United States

The gesture of the Way of the Cross proposed by Fr Giussani has spread around the world. On a cloudy and rainy Good Friday in 1996, a handful of friends belonging to Communion and Liberation in the United States made the first Way of the Cross over the Brooklyn Bridge. With the blessing of then Bishop of Brooklyn, H.E. Thomas V. Daily, and accompanied by Fr. Ronald Marino from the Diocese of Brooklyn, less than thirty people followed the Cross along the bridge that day. The gesture has continued for over 25 years, and last year, around three thousands people participated (Click here to learn more about the WoC in New York City).
